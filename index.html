We Know Training

2020—Brand, web, print



Overview: A company that delivers training online needs to stand out in a saturated market. This new company not only needed a new name, but a completely fresh identity. Something that showcased it’s professionalism and expertise, but at the same time represented it’s fun staff, engaging client relationships, and above all uniqueness.

Yardstick

2017—Web, print



Overview: Yardstick delivers online training as well as high-stakes testing. Being a full-service solution allowed Yardstick to stand out from the crowd on services alone, but a brand was needed to offset the amount of dull academic brands surrounding it. Mixing black and white colours, yellow accents, and both greyscale and full colour photography gave the brand a unique look that was hard to miss.

Y Station

2020—Branding, web



Overview: Y Station is a reimagined communications & research company focused on informing critical decision-making. In an industry that is often filled with stagnant, and boring, competition, Y Station needed a look to break a part from the crowd. The result was a simple black and white logo with a hidden speech bubble mark, signifying their work in communication and surveys.

Learner Verified

2017—UX/UI, web, print



Overview: LearnerVerified sets to take on an online training dilemma, learner fraud. With such a serious topic the brand needed to feel trustworthy, professional, and honest. Utilizing white as a dominant colour allowed for designs to feel light, with blue and green adding emotions of trustworthiness and verification.

A clear, easy-to-use, and simple interface was designed for both the desktop application of verifying a learner as well as a mobile app for managing digital certifications and credentials.

Banister Research

2018—Branding, web, print



Overview: A company with a history, Banister Research had been around the market research industry for over 20 years, without updating their brand. They needed a new brand to bring them into the modern world, with something that was bold and that set them apart from their past. The result was a brand that signified what they do, bringing data together.

Business Career College

2020—Branding



Overview: A company with a long history, BCC needed a modernization for their brand that felt more energetic, could attract a younger audience, and above all displayed personality. Utilizing blues gives a sense of professionalism, trust, and authority. The secondary colours are used to invoke energy and personality.

Local Waste

2016—Branding, web, print



Overview: Local Waste wanted to take on the big players in waste management, showing potential customers that they were just like them and best of all, local. Their logo needed to reflect recognizable imagery in their home province of Alberta, such as mountain ranges, wheat fields, and rolling hills. On top of this the brand itself needed to feel friendly and inviting.

eLearning

Overview: Clients required eLearning to deliver training and knowledge, ensuring that their learners gained competency. L2 Fitness

2018—Web design & development



Overview: In a market that was saturated with gyms of poor quality, bad service, and even worse reputation, L2 Fitness wanted a design that elevated them above and beyond what was in the local gym scene. The result was a design that matched their professional, fun, and modern business style.

It all started with a Pokémon fan site.

No, seriously.

When I was 11 I built my first website, a Pokémon fan site, using Microsoft Frontpage. I was hooked. I quickly started prioritizing design knowledge over video games, a big deal for a young kid. Although I did keep those two tied closely together, designing and developing websites for video game teams and clans.

The more I became aware of design, the more enthralled I became. Who knew there was more to it than “just making pretty things.” I started learning about principles, theory, and how people perceive anything visual.

From there I went on to MacEwan University, graduating with a major in Design & Illustration. Since that point I haven’t looked back, always growing my experiences and knowledge through a variety of projects and clients.
